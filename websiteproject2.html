Should we Tamper with Genetics?

By: Adrian Sheehy

Pros Cons Pros and Cons

How will this Benefit our Society?

Editing genes will allow people to become more resilient to disease, and stop birth defects. Scientists have began to learn the human genome, and can create GMOs in plants without any adverse side effects. Why should we stop with plants? The UN states that it is unethical to experiment on humans, as it is inhumane. However, this could allow us to fully understand our genes, and be able to make plan ahead with the new information we have gathered. People fear what they do not understand, and this scenario is no exception. With careful studies, we can successfully change ourselves to get rid of genetic defects such as: diabetes, heart disease, balding, epilepsy, and many others! With this new technology, we can make the next generation of humanity outlast us, and create a brighter future. Our human pancreas is irreplaceable, as every human has a different metabolism. If someone were to get pancreatic cancer, then they could die if it spreads. CRISPR has edited stem cells to create pancreatic tissue, and transplant the new parts to the original pancreas. It also has been used to treat sickle cell disorder. If we were to repeat this process with other organs of the body, humans could possibly become immortal, as they could transplant virtually every organ except for their brain.




How will this Prove Detrimental?

Recently, a Chinese scientist has edited the genes of certain children using CRISPR, and questions have arisen on whether or not this is an ethical decision. The scientist has claimed that the children are resistant to HIV, among other genes. Most likely, the children will die because of genetic defects created from editing certain genes. The United Nations currently deems editing human genetics as "inhumane." The scientist edited the gene CCR5, which is a gene used by white blood cells that open protein gateways for HIV retroviruses to enter. While this stops HIV viruses from injecting their DNA into the host cell, this also leaves the human body susceptible to other diseases because it weakens the body's immune system. What many people do not understand is that genetically modified humans also technically have human rights under United Nations law. If people tamper with genetics, the "superhumans" have the right to have children, and could corrupt our gene pool. This could lead to unknown consequences later on. Furthermore, according to genome.gov, cloned embryos had trouble developing, and the organisms later died. While editing genes could help with birth defects, we could not predict the consequences that are to come. Because of this, editing genes is too dangerous to attempt at this point in time. No human could ever fathom how someone would feel if they were told that they were changed because someone was not satisfied with who they originally were.
